About the Jay String Quartet

"A new voice in chamber music ..." BBC Radio 3

Who we are

The Jay String Quartet is recognised as making an important new contribution to chamber music. They came together as a quartet in London in 2016, and quickly formed a bond over a shared enthusiasm for the music of the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries.

Following success at the Montreal prix, they have performed at major venues in North America, Europe, and Japan.

We also work with composers and musicians in other genres, and are proud to have premiered a number of chamber pieces. We are devoted to all the arts and work with visual artists, choreographers, and writers in developing new work.

The quartet is enthusiastically devoted to music in the community in the widest sense. We are keen supporters of the Strings for Schools initiative, and work closely with primary and secondary pupils.

We are passionate about developing new voices, talents, and insights from different communities, and hope to inspire young people to become more actively involved in music, whatver the genre.

Meet the quartet

Hye-Jung Kang

Hje-Jung graduated from the Chan-Wook Conservatory in Seoul before studying with Celine Sciamma and Fatih Akin at the Guildhall School of Music.

Gilles Lelouche

Gilles first studied with Jeanne Herry in Lyons and Katell Quillévéré in Paris. He also performs with the Jacques Trio and with the Ensemble Marseille.

Leo Fan

Leo studied at the Hou Conservatoire in Taipei and at the Guildhall School of Music. He achieved success in the Taiwan International Strings Competition, and has performed around the world with the Concerto Elefantino.

Anne Dorval

Anne studied at the Quebec Ecole and in London at the Guildhall School of Music. She performed with the award-winning Demy Quintet, and teaches at the Royal College of Music.
